You can make a new account by going to the LingSync app.
What is the lexicon of my corpus and where does it come from?
The lexicon is automatically generated from the data in your corpus. This means that in order to add words to your lexicon, you must a datum in your corpus which includes this word. The reason for this is that the lexicon is modelled as a mental lexicon, meaning that it directly reflects the speaker's experience of hearing words in context.
How do I clean a lexical entry?
Click on the text that you would like to edit, make your change, and then click the save button It will show you your proposed changes for each datum and ask you if you would like to continue. If you click ok, your changes will be saved to all relevant data, and you will receive a confirmation message. If any of the data fails to saved, you will receive a warning message.
How do I view lexical entries which need to be cleaned?
The opacity of each item in the lexicon indicates the confidence of the lexical entry, which means the amount of times the item has been entered and glossed consistently in the corpus. Low-confidence items are more likely in need of cleaning. Therefore, the view can be filtered to show a particular confidence range, e.g. 0.1 - 0.5 for items which most likely need cleaning, or 0.7 - 1 for items which most likely don't need cleaning. To filter the view to a particular confidence range, you can move the sliders in the top menue towards the minimum and maximum confidence that you would like to see displayed.
If you are just looking for a particular item to clean, you can simply find my by searching for it in the lexical entries (using control + f).
